Resources & Archives

This is your directory to the infrastructure of zine culture—where to find zines, where to learn more, where to donate your own work, and what tools and communities exist to support zine-making. Resources change constantly as organizations fold, websites disappear, and new projects emerge. Consider this a snapshot that will require updating, but these are the major players and institutions as of 2025.

Note on Link Rot: The internet is ephemeral. Websites disappear, URLs change, projects end. If a resource listed here is no longer active when you're reading this, that's the nature of DIY culture and the internet. Search for current alternatives, ask in zine communities, or check the Internet Archive's Wayback Machine for historical information.

Zine Libraries and Archives

These institutions collect, preserve, and provide access to zines. Many lend zines like regular libraries. Others are reference collections where you can read but not check out. Supporting them (through donations of zines or money) helps preserve zine culture.

Barnard Zine Library

New York, NY | barnard.edu/library/zines

One of the most significant zine collections in the world, housed at Barnard College. Focus on feminist, gender, and sexuality-related zines, but collection is broad. Open to the public by appointment. Accepts donations. Extensive online catalog makes searching easy.

Denver Zine Library

Denver, CO | denverzinelibrary.org

A lending library specifically for zines. You can browse, check out zines, and attend events. Volunteer-run and community-focused. Model for what a local zine library can be.

Queer Zine Archive Project (QZAP)

Milwaukee, WI | gittings.org/qzap

Preserves and provides access to queer zines. Extensive collection documenting LGBTQ+ zine history. Traveling shows bring zines to different cities. Important resource for queer zine history and culture.

ABC No Rio Zine Library

New York, NY | abcnorio.org

Part of the anarchist community center ABC No Rio, this library focuses on political, radical, and activist zines. Reflects the political history of zine culture. Open during center hours.

Salt ydog Zine Library

Portland, OR | saltydogzinelibrary.tumblr.com

Portland's zine library, reflecting the city's strong zine scene. Lending library with regular events and workshops. Community gathering space for zine culture.

Aside: Local Zine Collections Many public and university libraries have zine collections even if they're not dedicated zine libraries. Check your local library's special collections. Increasingly, libraries see zines as valuable cultural documents worth preserving. Some will even accept donations for their collections.
