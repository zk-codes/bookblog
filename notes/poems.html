Top Poetry Blogs, Websites & Poetry Bloggers To Follow

Written By: Zachary Kai » Published: 13 Feb 2020 | Updated: 26 Aug 2023

Seeking the top poetry blogs on the internet to satisfy your curiosity? I've got you covered! Discover the best poetry websites in this curated list.

Credit: Christine Neale

Excellent Poetry Blogs To Follow

A

  • AG Leyden: A personal poetry blog featuring original verse and reflections on the craft of poetry writing.
  • Anachronizms: Poetry blog exploring themes that sit outside of linear time, with experimental and contemplative verse.
  • Anomalocrinus Incurvus: A unique poetry space named after a prehistoric sea creature, featuring distinctive and thought-provoking poems.
  • Archetype: Poetry exploring archetypal patterns, symbols, and the recurring themes that shape human experience.
  • As In Guillotine: Bold, visceral poetry that doesn't shy away from sharp edges and difficult truths.
  • Average Poet: Unpretentious verse celebrating the everyday experience of writing poetry without pretension.
  • Axis Of Abraxas: Jessica Goodfellow's poetry blog featuring her work and insights into the creative process.

B

  • Bartholomew Barker: Personal site of poet Bartholomew Barker, showcasing his poetry, publications, and literary projects.
  • Being Poetry: A blog exploring what it means to exist through the lens of poetry, with philosophical undertones.
  • Belz: A contemporary poetry blog featuring original work and commentary on the current poetry landscape.
  • Best American Poetry, The: Showcases exceptional contemporary American poetry with essays, selected poems, and interviews. The blog presents work from various poetry journals alongside critical writing on the art form. Essential reading for anyone wanting to keep up with current American poetry.
  • Breathing Silver Linings: Hopeful, optimistic poetry finding light in difficult circumstances and celebrating resilience.
  • Bugolgi Apparatus: Experimental poetry blog with an unusual name, exploring unconventional forms and subjects.

C

  • Chicks Dig Poetry: An inviting poetry space celebrating the diversity of voices and perspectives in contemporary verse.
  • Confessions Of A Laundry Goddess: Poetry infused with domestic life, finding beauty and meaning in everyday moments and routines.
  • Cynthia Cruz: Personal blog of poet Cynthia Cruz, featuring her poems and reflections on poetry and culture.

D

  • Dating A Poet: A unique perspective on life, love, and poetry, written from the intimate vantage point of poet relationships.

E

  • Eat This Poem: A delightful intersection of poetry and food, pairing poems with recipes and culinary inspiration.
  • Ebbortz: Personal poetry blog featuring original work with a distinctive voice and style.
  • Erica Goss: Official site of poet Erica Goss, featuring her published work, news, and poetry resources.

F

  • Fleda Brown: Award-winning poet Fleda Brown's blog, offering insights into craft, process, and the poet's life.
  • Free Minds Book Club: Features powerful poetry written by incarcerated young people in the United States. Free Minds works with inmates to help them discover their creative voices and share their work. The poems are raw, honest, and demonstrate remarkable talent despite difficult circumstances. Readers can leave encouraging feedback that's passed directly to the poets.
  • Friday Influence, The: Weekly poetry posts exploring various influences, inspirations, and the forces that shape creative work.

G

  • Giganticjet: Expansive poetry blog with ambitious scope, exploring big ideas through verse.

H

  • HG Poetics: Poetry blog focusing on the technical and theoretical aspects of poetic craft and composition.
  • How A Poem Happens: Brian Brodeur interviews accomplished poets about their creative process, offering fascinating behind-the-scenes insights into how individual poems are crafted. The blog reveals the often hidden work of revision, inspiration, and decision-making that goes into poetry. Perfect for writers wanting to learn about process and readers curious about the mechanics of poetry.

I

  • Inkked Solace: Poetry offering comfort and solace through the written word, exploring themes of healing and peace.

J

  • Jeremy Cantor: Poet Jeremy Cantor's blog featuring his work, publications, and reflections on the poetry world.
  • John Davis Jr: Personal site of poet John Davis Jr, showcasing his poetry collections and literary activities.
  • Joy Leftow's Poetry Blog: Poet Joy Leftow shares her original poetry and thoughts on the art of writing verse.

L

  • Language Like Medicine: Ann Kestner's blog exploring poetry's healing power, treating language as a therapeutic and transformative force.
  • Last Bender: Poetry section of a multidisciplinary creative blog, featuring verse alongside other artistic work.
  • Life In Technicolor: Vibrant, colorful poetry celebrating life's richness and exploring diverse emotional landscapes.

M

  • Magma Poetry: A quarterly magazine based in the UK, with each issue centered around a specific theme. Features both submitted and curated contemporary poetry alongside interviews, articles, and reviews. Available in print and digital formats, with poetry competitions and workshops throughout the year. Essential for readers who want themed collections and high-quality contemporary British poetry.
  • McCarra: Personal poetry blog featuring original verse and poetic experimentation.
  • Meta Phore Play: Michael Dickel's poetry exploring metaphor, language play, and the philosophical dimensions of verse.
  • Motivation Effect: Inspirational poetry designed to uplift, encourage, and motivate readers through difficult times.

O

  • Old News: Ryan Eckes' poetry blog featuring work that engages with history, memory, and the passage of time.
  • Oliver De La Paz: Acclaimed poet Oliver de la Paz shares insights on craft, publishing, and the literary life.

P

  • Pangrammaticon, The: A poetry blog celebrating wordplay, language, and the joy of linguistic experimentation.
  • Poemattic: Melba Christie's poetry space in the attic, featuring intimate, contemplative verse.
  • Poet Mom: Poetry from the perspective of motherhood, balancing creative work with family life.
  • Poetry And Miscellaneous Yap: An informal poetry blog mixing verse with personal commentary and musings.
  • Poetry For The Great Turning: Karina Lutz's poetry addressing environmental and social transformation, focused on positive change.
  • Poetry Foundation: The Poetry Foundation runs Poetry Magazine, the oldest English-language verse publication in the world. As the largest and oldest poetry organization, they offer extensive archives, educational resources, and contemporary poetry coverage. An essential resource for any serious poetry reader.
  • Poetry Project, The: Founded in 1960s New York with weekly poetry gatherings, The Poetry Project has grown into a vital literary institution. They publish The Recluse literary magazine, host events and workshops, and maintain extensive archives of readings and performances. A hub for experimental and innovative poetry, especially for readers interested in the New York School tradition and contemporary avant-garde work.
  • Poetry Shed, The: Abegail Morley's poetry blog, a cozy space for sharing verse and craft insights.
  • Poetrymind: A blog exploring the intersection of poetry and consciousness, thought and verse.
  • Poets And Writers: Nonprofit serving the literary community since 1970, offering programs, a magazine, prizes, and extensive resources for poets and writers. Their blogs feature writing advice, publishing guidance, and literary news. They support poetry bloggers and provide tools for the writing life. An invaluable resource for anyone serious about poetry as craft or career.
  • Poets United: A vibrant community where poets share work, interact, and connect through their love of poetry. The site features diverse content from talented contributors and fosters genuine poetic community. Perfect for poets seeking feedback, inspiration, and connection with other writers.
  • Possum: Poetry blog featuring fragmented, experimental verse exploring unconventional forms.

S

  • Savage Love Poetry: Run by two anonymous poets, this blog explores the duality of human nature—our capacity for both creation and destruction. The poetry is raw, lyrical, and bittersweet, capturing intense emotional landscapes. Perfect for readers drawn to visceral, unfiltered emotional truth in verse.
  • Seventeenfingeredpoetrybird: Whimsically named poetry blog featuring imaginative, surreal verse.
  • Seth Abramson: Poet Seth Abramson's work, known for innovative forms and engagement with contemporary issues.
  • Sherry Chandler Poet: Personal site of poet Sherry Chandler, featuring her published work and poetry news.
  • Smoke Words Every Day: Daily poetry and reflections, creating a consistent practice of poetic expression.
  • Something Katy: Personal poetry blog with an approachable voice and relatable themes.
  • South Broadway Ghost Society: A poetry society and literary journal highlighting exceptional poets from the New York area. The poems tend toward emotive and dark themes with multiple contributors. They maintain a list of publication opportunities for poets seeking outlets for their work. Valuable for both reading contemporary urban poetry and for poets looking for submission calls.

T

  • TheClosureSessions: Poetry exploring endings, conclusions, and the process of finding closure.
  • Tom Clark: Renowned poet Tom Clark's blog, offering decades of poetic wisdom and craft.

U

  • Ursprache: Poetry blog exploring original language, etymology, and the roots of expression.

V

  • Very Most Good: Poetry celebrating optimism, goodness, and the positive aspects of human experience.
  • Vihang A Naik: Poet Vihang A Naik's official site featuring his published poetry and literary work.

W

  • War Is Over, The: A series of narrative poems set in 2086, depicting a future world transformed by technology. The poetry employs lyrical rhyming and descriptive language to create vivid world-building through verse. Perfect for readers who enjoy science fiction poetry and speculative writing.
  • White Words: Naoko Fujimoto's poetry blog featuring minimalist, contemplative verse.
  • Wild About Poetry: Mary Robinson's enthusiastic celebration of poetry in all its forms.
  • Winding Sheets: Poetry blog exploring mortality, memory, and the passage of time.
  • Worldly Winds: Poetry inspired by travel, cultures, and the interconnectedness of global experience.
